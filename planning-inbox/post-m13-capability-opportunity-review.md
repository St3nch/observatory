# Post-M13 Capability and Commercial Opportunity Review

Status: planning review / audit response
Authority: none; does not change roadmap, admit providers, authorize spend, approve capture, or authorize implementation
Date: 2026-07-12
Source: `audits/observatory-post-m13-deep-audit-2026-07-12.md`, sections 12–13
Milestone: M14 planning

---

## Purpose

Review every O-1 through O-10 opportunity from the post-M13 audit so none is lost, overpromoted, or treated as automatic roadmap authority.

The review asks for each opportunity:

- what evidence capability it would create;
- why it could matter commercially;
- what boundary keeps Observatory from becoming the strategy engine;
- which rights, cost, methodology, provider, or persistence gates apply;
- where it belongs in the milestone/research sequence;
- what is allowed now.

---

## Governing Rule

```text
The moat is the longitudinal, provenance-complete, rights-clean observation corpus and the joins it permits.
The LLM interprets at read time.
Accepted meaning promotes out.
```

An opportunity is valuable only if it preserves that law.

No opportunity below authorizes:

- a provider request;
- recurring capture;
- schema or Postgres;
- customer data;
- report generation;
- strategy or recommendation storage;
- automated spend;
- marketplace scraping;
- production API/MCP.

---

## Classification Vocabulary

### Active M14 design input

The opportunity directly affects read-contract shape now without requiring the underlying future evidence family to exist.

### Future milestone candidate

The opportunity fits an existing later milestone and should be preserved as acceptance/planning input for that milestone.

### Research activation candidate

The opportunity is valuable but cannot be responsibly planned until named methodology, rights, provider, or cadence research is activated.

### Deferred behind hard gate

The opportunity remains parked until an owner ruling or source-family admission clears it.

## O-1 — Multi-provider disagreement maps

Disposition: **future milestone candidate — M16**

Value:

- High differentiation: side-by-side provider testimony with receipts instead of declaring a truth provider.
- Useful to SearchClarity, internal research, and future consumer analysis.
- Directly operationalizes the project’s provider-testimony doctrine.

Boundary:

- Store provider-attributed observations only.
- No winner field.
- No averaged truth score.
- Any disagreement description is compute-on-read unless OR-A1 later permits a narrowly mechanical materialization.

Prerequisites:

- M16 Provider Cross-Check Proof;
- second admitted provider or second comparable engine/surface;
- metric-identity and comparability rules from RG9/DR3;
- provider-specific rights/cost admission.

Allowed now:

- M14 may ensure read packs can represent multiple provider-attribution blocks and incomparability warnings.

Not allowed now:

- provider expansion;
- Ahrefs/Semrush work;
- comparison persistence;
- winner/ranking logic.

## O-2 — SERP feature evolution and volatility evidence

Disposition: **future milestone candidate — M18, with DR12 methodology input**

Value:

- Longitudinal feature appearance/disappearance, rank churn, domain diversity, and result-family composition are unusually valuable evidence.
- Helps ground freshness and volatility classes in measured behavior rather than folklore.

Boundary:

- Mechanical diffs only.
- No causal explanation or tactical recommendation stored.
- Volatility labels must be derived from declared sampling windows and must remain caveated.

Prerequisites:

- repeated/recurring capture authority;
- cadence and cost research under DR12;
- canonical fingerprint/item-family behavior;
- M18 recurring watch planning;
- rights/retention posture per source family.

Allowed now:

- M14 may include historical/as-of and volatility metadata fields in read-contract planning.

Not allowed now:

- recurring capture;
- automatic volatility-triggered pulls;
- claims from one C00 specimen.

## O-3 — AI-citation / GEO surface comparison and citation asymmetry

Disposition: **research activation candidate and future M15/M16 capability**

Value:

- Highest near-term commercial differentiation for SearchClarity.
- Dated, sampled, source-attributed AI citation observations could support unusually honest GEO evidence products.
- Citation asymmetry is useful when stated as observed sampled difference, not market truth.

Boundary:

- Distinct AI surfaces/families must never be collapsed.
- Absence claims require repeated sampling and explicit scope limits.
- No influence, causality, or recommendation inference stored.
- Customer-facing language remains SearchClarity-owned.

Prerequisites:

- DR4 GEO/AI citation methodology;
- DR5 Google AI surface limits;
- OR-C3 provider-family admission;
- repeated-sampling thresholds under OR-E3;
- rights/cost review for each instrument;
- later M15/M16 proof.

Allowed now:

- preserve as a top-priority research lane;
- M14 read packs must support surface identity, prompt/panel version, captured time, sample limits, citations/mentions, and absence warnings.

Not allowed now:

- AI endpoint pull;
- customer-facing AI visibility claim;
- recurring AI monitoring.

## O-4 — Entity/source recurrence across answer engines

Disposition: **future derived capability after O-3**

Value:

- Domain/source recurrence across surfaces and dates is a strong mechanical observation join.
- Could support the safe statement that a source was repeatedly observed, without claiming trust, authority, or influence.

Boundary:

- Start at domain/source recurrence, not broad entity inference.
- Any persisted rollup must pass the V6 materialization test.
- “Trusted by AI” is forbidden unless quoted as provider testimony; recurrence is not trust.

Prerequisites:

- O-3 evidence families;
- stable source/domain normalization;
- repeated samples;
- explicit materialization/read-time decision.

Allowed now:

- preserve as downstream opportunity.

Not allowed now:

- entity graph;
- trust score;
- influence score;
- recurrence materialization.

## O-5 — Public-page change lineage

Disposition: **deferred research and source-family admission candidate**

Value:

- Hash-linked snapshots and mechanical diffs could preserve evidence competitors rarely retain.
- Supports future as-of analysis and intervention alignment without storing strategy.

Boundary:

- Public-page snapshots are observations only.
- Diff summaries must remain mechanical.
- No claim that a page change caused a ranking change.
- Per-source rights, robots/terms, retention, and capture-instrument posture govern admission.

Prerequisites:

- DR2 raw retention;
- DR8 manual/browser capture admissibility;
- source-specific rights review;
- OR-C4 archive layout;
- an admitted snapshot family/instrument;
- later persistence planning.

Allowed now:

- preserve as a future family candidate.

Not allowed now:

- scraping;
- Firecrawl admission;
- browser-extension capture;
- snapshot storage.

## O-6 — Marketplace visibility evidence at the compliant ceiling

Disposition: **deferred behind marketplace rights and manual-capture rulings**

Value:

- Rights-clean, point-in-time marketplace evidence could be commercially useful precisely because compliant longitudinal evidence is scarce.
- Supports SearchClarity’s likely marketplace services.

Boundary:

- The compliance ceiling is part of the product.
- Manual public observation may be the maximum lawful/acceptable instrument.
- Marketplace observations require brutal point-in-time caveats.
- Customer records and private marketplace analytics remain outside Observatory.

Prerequisites:

- DR6 Etsy;
- DR7 Fiverr;
- DR8 manual/browser capture;
- OR-C5 through OR-C8;
- future manual-capture family contract;
- M15 customer-language validation.

Allowed now:

- preserve as a high-value future SearchClarity lane.

Not allowed now:

- marketplace scraping;
- automated capture;
- browser extension;
- marketplace evidence storage.

## O-7 — Query-panel coverage/blind-spot plus cost-to-close

Disposition: **active M14 design input; later panel/provider metadata implementation**

Value:

- States what is missing, which surfaces/providers were not observed, and the observed provider cost of filling a gap.
- Converts uncertainty into transparent evidence and cost facts without recommending spend.

Boundary:

- Read output may state coverage gaps and known provider pricing metadata.
- It must not recommend a purchase, create a capture job, or spend money.
- Provider pricing is time-stamped testimony, not a permanent price truth.

Prerequisites:

- query-panel activation in later work;
- provider pricing evidence model;
- bounded coverage vocabulary;
- no-spend read boundary.

Allowed now:

- M14 read contract should include coverage/blind-spot sections and optional observed-cost metadata.

Not allowed now:

- capture request queue;
- cost recommendation;
- automated gap filling.

## O-8 — Historical observation replay / as-of reads

Disposition: **active M14 design input; future persistence implementation**

Value:

- Enables reproducible “what was known then” reads, dispute resolution, and durable governance citations.
- Cheap to design early and expensive to retrofit.

Boundary:

- As-of reads reconstruct evidence state, not historical strategy or conclusions.
- Superseded/withdrawn/expired statuses must remain visible and correctly resolved.

Prerequisites:

- OR-D3 status semantics;
- append-only/supersession-capable persistence later;
- stable evidence handles;
- deterministic read ordering.

Allowed now:

- include `as_of`/historical-use behavior in M14 contract planning.

Not allowed now:

- persistence implementation;
- fabricated historical reconstruction from incomplete fixtures.

## O-9 — Freshness-aware LLM context packs

Disposition: **core M14 capability**

Value:

- This is the primary way the telescope safely empowers the astronomer.
- Makes strong LLMs useful without giving them SQL, raw rows, detachable caveats, or hidden scope leakage.

Boundary:

- Evidence and caveats remain adjacent.
- Stored observation content is untrusted data, not instruction text.
- Token limits drop whole evidence units with explicit truncation metadata; warnings are never stripped.
- Interpretive conclusions are not persisted back into Observatory.

Prerequisites:

- M14 contract acceptance;
- OR-D1 through OR-D5 rulings or explicit fail-closed defaults;
- hostile-path and contract-acceptance tests;
- local fixture prototype authorization later.

Allowed now:

- full contract and hostile-path planning.

Not allowed now:

- production MCP/API;
- customer-facing report generation;
- direct SQL.

## O-10 — Low-cost sentinel campaigns and rare-feature capture

Disposition: **future M18+ candidate behind the strongest spend/capture gates**

Boundary:

- M18 planning;
- clone-stable, decision-linked spend authority;
- cumulative and post-receipt cost controls;
- canonical drift/family detection;
- source-family retention ruling;
- explicit automated-trigger owner ruling.

Allowed now:

- preserve the concept and requirements.

Not allowed now:

- standing budget;
- event-triggered pull;
- autonomous spend;
- recurring capture.
