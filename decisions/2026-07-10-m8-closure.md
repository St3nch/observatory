## Context

M8 was opened after M7 closed and all thirteen planned M7 non-schema contracts were drafted and indexed.

M8's purpose was to define hostile-path hammer categories and acceptance gates before first-slice selection, schema planning, implementation, provider admission, typed read tools, consumer workflows, or recurring capture planning.

M8 produced:

- `hammers/README.md`
- `hammers/hammer-matrix-v0-1.md`
- `hammers/acceptance-gate-policy-v0-1.md`
- `planning-inbox/m8-hammer-planning-review.md`

The M8 review found the hammer matrix and acceptance-gate policy strong enough for M9 to compare first-slice candidates without guessing acceptance criteria.

---

## Options Considered

| Option | Summary | Result | Reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Keep M8 open until OR-B1, OR-B2, and OR-B3 receive final owner rulings | rejected | This would stall first-slice definition even though M8 has fail-closed defaults sufficient for planning |
| B | Close M8 and treat OR-B1, OR-B2, and OR-B3 as fully ruled | rejected | That would silently promote draft policy into binding owner decisions |
| C | Close M8 with OR-B1, OR-B2, and OR-B3 open but explicitly fail-closed | accepted | Preserves safety while allowing M9 to begin first-slice definition |

---

## Scope

This decision applies to:

- closing M8 as a planning milestone;
- activating M9 for first evidence slice definition;
- carrying M8 hammer outputs forward as M9 candidate filters;
- preserving OR-B1 through OR-B3 as open/fail-closed rather than silently decided.

This decision does not apply to:

- schema design;
- migrations;
- implementation;
- provider admission;
- provider purchases;
- paid provider pulls;
- API/MCP implementation;
- dashboard work;
- customer-data handling;
- capture runner work;
- automated recurring capture;
- strategy or recommendation storage;
- marking any hammer as executed or passed.
